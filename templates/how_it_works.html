METHODOLOGY

How Cipher Lens thinks.

Cipher Lens scores every stock against five signal categories drawn from a structured investing framework. Here's the high-level methodology — the principles, not the formulas.

01

Trend matters more than feelings

A stock above its 50-day and 200-day moving averages is in an uptrend. A stock below both is in a downtrend. Cipher Lens checks where the price sits relative to its trend lines, whether the trend structure is healthy, and how close the stock is to its 52-week high.

02

Momentum has guardrails

The RSI (Relative Strength Index) tells us if a stock is overbought, oversold, or in a healthy range. Cipher Lens favors stocks with momentum in a sustainable band — not overheated, not collapsing — and it pays attention to the direction RSI is moving.

03

Volume confirms or contradicts

Big moves on weak volume are suspicious. Real conviction shows up as participation. Cipher Lens compares today's volume to the recent average to assess whether the current price action has real weight behind it.

04

Sectors rise and fall together

A great stock in a falling sector is still a stock in a falling sector. Cipher Lens checks whether each ticker's sector is outperforming or underperforming the S&P 500 over the past three weeks. Stocks in sectors with strong tailwinds get a more favorable read; stocks in sectors with heavy headwinds get downgraded.

05

Fundamentals anchor the analysis

Technical signals alone aren't enough. Cipher Lens looks at analyst price targets (is there meaningful upside?), market capitalization (is this a serious company?), and analyst consensus (do the pros lean bullish or bearish?). These don't dominate the score — but they prevent chasing strong-looking charts on companies the market has soured on.

06

Earnings windows are no-fly zones

When a company reports earnings within the next 21 days, the score downgrades automatically. Earnings reactions are unpredictable. Cipher Lens treats the blackout window as a strong "wait until after the report" signal — even for otherwise strong-looking stocks.

What the ratings mean

GREEN — Strong

Most categories pass. The stock has multiple favorable signals working together. Still: do your own research.

YELLOW — Mixed

Some signals positive, others concerning. Worth a closer look, but not a clear winner. Often these stocks reward patience or need a catalyst.

RED — Weak

Multiple concerning signals. Could be a downtrend, weak sector, earnings blackout, or fundamental weakness. Worth knowing why before considering an entry.

NO DATA

Could not fetch enough data to score. Often a misspelled ticker, a stock that's been delisted, or a thinly-traded name.

How to use Cipher Lens well

A screening tool is only as good as the discipline of the person using it. Here are habits worth adopting — true for any framework, not just this one.

Treat green as a starting point, not a destination

A green rating means the framework saw multiple favorable signals together. It doesn't mean the stock will go up. Use the rating to decide where to spend your research time — then actually do the research.

Look at the chart yourself

Cipher Lens checks where the price sits relative to moving averages and the 52-week high. It can't see chart patterns, support and resistance levels, or how the stock behaved in past corrections. Pull up the actual chart on a free tool (TradingView, Yahoo Finance) and look at the last 6–12 months before you decide.

Read the last earnings call before buying anything

Earnings transcripts (free on Seeking Alpha and most company investor relations pages) tell you what management actually said about the business — and what analysts pressed them on. A green stock with weak guidance and analysts asking hard questions is a different proposition than a green stock with strong commentary.

Check news within the last week

Cipher Lens uses delayed market data. Major news in the last few days — earnings preannouncements, regulatory actions, management changes, sector-wide events — may not yet be reflected in the score. A 30-second news search on the ticker can save you from buying into a story that's already broken.

Diversify across sectors, not just stocks

If your green list is five tech stocks, you don't have five positions — you have one bet on tech with five tickers. Real diversification means different sectors with different macro drivers. A green rating in a tech stock plus a green rating in healthcare is more durable than two greens in the same sector.

Use position sizing that lets you survive being wrong

No framework is right every time. Cipher Lens will produce green ratings on stocks that go down. The question isn't whether you'll be wrong — it's whether your position size lets you stay in the game when you are. If a single position would meaningfully damage your capital if it dropped 20%, the position is too big regardless of how good the score looks.

Don't act on the same day you scored

The signals Cipher Lens checks change slowly. A stock that's green today will almost certainly still be green tomorrow. Sleeping on a decision is one of the most underrated forms of risk management available to retail investors. It costs nothing and prevents most impulse mistakes.

Important limitations

What this tool doesn't know

Cipher Lens scores stocks. It does NOT consider:

  • Your portfolio composition or how a new position fits
  • Your risk tolerance, time horizon, or tax situation
  • Macroeconomic events not yet reflected in price
  • News in the last 24 hours (data lags)
  • Insider activity, short interest, or unusual options flow
  • Industry-specific factors that require human judgment

A green rating doesn't mean buy. A red rating doesn't mean sell. They mean: "according to this framework, the technical and fundamental profile looks favorable / unfavorable right now." How you use that information is up to you.

Data sources

Price data, technicals, sector classification, analyst targets, and earnings dates come from Yahoo Finance via the yfinance library. Data may be delayed 15–20 minutes during market hours. The tool has no special access to real-time feeds or insider information.
